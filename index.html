What I can help with

People come to counselling for many reasons. You might be feeling anxious, low, overwhelmed, stuck, or simply unsure why things don’t feel right.

  • Anxiety, stress and feeling overwhelmed
  • Depression and low mood
  • Low self-esteem and confidence
  • Autism, ADHD and neurodiversity
  • Loss, bereavement and life transitions
  • Trauma and difficult experiences

About me

I run a private practice offering online sessions, and face-to-face counselling in Horndean, Hampshire. I also work for I’m All Ears counselling service in Surrey.

I am a member of the British Association for Counselling and Psychotherapy (BACP) and I work within their ethical framework for good practice.

How I work

Everyone experiences challenges, and sometimes we need additional support to navigate and overcome them. As a trained psychotherapist, I offer a professional and skilled approach through a process of self-exploration and discovery within the therapeutic relationship.

My aim is to help you explore and understand your thoughts, feelings and behaviours in order to support healing and growth.

Your first session: before you decide to proceed, I offer a complimentary 20-minute phone call so we can talk through what you’re looking for and any questions you may have.

Sessions: appointments are typically weekly at a consistent time. Sessions are 50 minutes, and we’ll regularly review how the work is feeling for you.

Practical details

I work online and in person from a private and confidential setting in Horndean. Many people find online sessions highly effective because you can meet from a familiar space, and travel can be difficult or anxiety-provoking.
